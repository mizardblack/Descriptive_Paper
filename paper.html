Basic Information
Magis Spun Chair is designed by Thomas Heatherwick and manufactured by Magis in 2010. It is a spinning-top-liked chair made out of rotationally molded polyethylene. The material is strong enough to hold up to 220 pounds of object on it. The size of the chair is 36” for width, 36" for depth, and 26" for height. Magis Spun Chairs sale for $ 895.00 and come with four colors: red, white, dark purple, and grey anthracite. Several of the samples of Magis Spun Chairs are currently displayed in Cooper Hewitt Smithsonian Design Museum.
About the Material
Polycarbonates are a group of thermoplastic polymers containing carbonate groups in their chemical structures. Polycarbonates used in engineering are strong, tough materials, and some grades are optically transparent. They are easily worked, molded, and thermoformed. Thus the chairs are light and strong.
User Experience
These black chairs are displayed in the empty corridor of the basement, echoing the grey carpet and the pure white wall. It's good to see

Just like a toy top, the bottom of the chair is also cone-shaped. Gradually widen upward, there is a tapered waist in the middle, like the bottleneck of a vase. Above the waist, in the center of the trumpet-like top, there is an inward depression, which is where people sit. The fine texture of the chair surface is caused by the polyethylene winded layer by layer.

The chairs displayed in the corridor not only serve as furniture for rest, but also as decorations and toys. Although spun chairs have many functions, their design is very simple: the back, legs and face of the chair are integrated without insignificant details.

Office chairs have elastic backs for alleviating back pain caused by sedentary working hours; student chairs are simple and strong for bulk purchase; sofas are soft and spacious for rest. As Horatio Greenough said, “form follows function”, spun chairs were made for fun. The cone-shape bottoms serve the function as toys.

Additionally, design guides users’ behavior. The back of the office chair leans forward to make the staffs close to their desks and improve work efficiency; the student chairs are hard, forcing the students to concentrate; the soft sofa makes people want to lie down unconsciously. The spun chairs lead people having fun on it, but they won’t lay down and take a nap because there is no back. Also, the chairs’ unique exteriors give people a strong hint that they are supposed to be put in the living room as ornaments.

At first, I felt that the design without the back of the chair was a flaw, because when I wanted to rest and lean back, there was nothing to support my head, so I had to sit upright. However, when my friend complained about the inconvenience of his new high-tech kettle, I realized that the extra design might not be a good one. My friend said that although his kettle could connect to his cell phone via Bluetooth, he was confused by the additional features. "I don't have time to play with a kettle," he said. Dieter Rams's mention that good designs are streamlined in his design principles. Therefore, spun chairs eliminates redundant functions and restricts the use of chairs to sitting straight and rotating, which is actually a smart design.
Conclusion
Magis Spun Chair is a typical postmodernism design object which allows the users using it in creative ways, made out of high-tech material and novel structure, and blend in with marketing strategy of selling different colors.
